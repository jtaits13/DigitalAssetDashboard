Week in brief

On balance, on-chain rail metrics held steadier than crypto spot markets—TradFi-bridge and tokenization coverage kept building momentum in the headlines.

 
•Tokenized MMFs — Institutional cash and settlement rail was little changed (-1.6% distributed value, 30D); issuer headlines stayed active (asset managers extending tokenized money-market funds into on-chain workflows); Ethereum still anchors ~52% of TMMF distributed value, and the network mix barely shifted.
•Stablecoins — Payments and reserve infrastructure was little changed (-0.7% market cap, 30D); issuer and bank-integration headlines outweighed the flat market-cap read this week.
•RWA tokenization — TradFi-to-on-chain bridge was little changed (-1.1% tokenized Treasuries, 30D); tokenization stayed in the news (Bank of England backs down on strict stablecoin holding), with TradFi distribution and collateral plumbing still the bottleneck.
•U.S. crypto ETFs — Spot ETF AUM softened (-24.2% aggregate AUM, 30D); crypto spot weakness hit AUM marks while a new listed product launched (BlackRock launched BITA, a covered-call Bitcoin ETF aimed at monthly income—listed crypto access is broadening beyond spot beta)—watch whether allocators rotate into income-oriented ETFs on the dip.
•Crypto prices — Crypto markets pulled back (-19.0% total market cap, 30D); on-chain rails were largely unchanged—suggesting macro and ETF flows, not rail fundamentals, drove the selloff.

  • TMMFs are becoming a de facto settlement layer for institutions.

    Several of the largest funds combine very high AUM with small holder counts—e.g. USYC, iBENJI, and JTRSY at roughly $46–67M per holder—suggesting on-chain cash and collateral rails rather than broad retail savings products. Ethereum still carries ~52% of aggregated MMF network share, alongside selective deployment on chains used for institutional workflows.

  • Early "flight to efficiency" shows up in TMMF 30D network share.

    Among tokenized money market funds, gainers include Stellar (+2.46% 30D TMMF network share), Solana (+2.37% 30D TMMF network share); losers include BNB Chain (-4.35% 30D TMMF network share), Ethereum (-2.31% 30D TMMF network share). That pattern is consistent with TMMF issuers routing more distributed value toward higher-throughput, lower-fee chains—though Ethereum still dominates TMMF share levels in most snapshots.
