Week in brief

•Tokenized MMFs — Institutional cash and settlement rail was little changed (-0.9% distributed value, 30D); issuer headlines stayed active (asset managers extending tokenized money-market funds into on-chain workflows); Ethereum still anchors ~52% of TMMF distributed value, and the network mix barely shifted.
•Stablecoins — Payments and reserve infrastructure was little changed (-1.3% market cap, 30D); bank-led issuance led the week (Former Tether investment chief is looking to sell part), suggesting issuance is running ahead of the flat market-cap read.
•RWA tokenization — TradFi-to-on-chain bridge was little changed (+0.5% tokenized Treasuries, 30D); watch securitization and broker-dealer distribution deals for the next leg of scale.
•U.S. crypto ETFs — Spot ETF AUM firmed (+6.4% aggregate AUM, 30D); flows and filings stayed active—monitor whether new product approvals translate into sustained AUM rebuild.
•Crypto prices — Crypto markets advanced (+1.8% total market cap, 30D); oversight headlines shared attention with prices (U.S. SEC to propose crypto rule), but policy noise was not the main market driver.

Key takeaways

  • TMMFs are becoming a de facto settlement layer for institutions.

    Several of the largest funds combine very high AUM with small holder counts—e.g. USYC (47 holders, $3.11B; ~$66M/holder), iBENJI (33 holders, $1.60B; ~$48M/holder), JTRSY (21 holders, $881M; ~$42M/holder)—suggesting on-chain cash and collateral rails rather than broad retail savings products. Ethereum still carries ~52% of aggregated MMF network share, alongside selective deployment on chains used for institutional workflows.

     

    Related reading · JPMorgan's JLTXX Tokenized Money Market Fund AUM Grows 250% in a Month on… · The Defiant

     

  • A global regulatory arbitrage map is emerging.

    Distributed value clusters into distinct compliance regimes—U.S. Reg S ~39% of population AUM (e.g. USYC, USDY); U.S. Reg D ~35% of population AUM (e.g. BUIDL, iBENJI); U.S. Mutual fund (N-1A) ~6% of population AUM (e.g. WTGXX, FILQ). Issuers appear to anchor products in familiar safe harbors rather than one global standard.
