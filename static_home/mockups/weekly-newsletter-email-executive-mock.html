Week in brief

On balance, on-chain rail metrics held steadier than crypto spot markets—bank-led stablecoin and payments-rail headlines kept advancing in the news flow.

•Tokenized MMFs — Institutional cash and settlement rail was little changed (-1.6% distributed value, 30D); issuer headlines stayed active (Franklin Templeton extended its BENJI tokenized money-market fund into new on-chain payment workflows); settlement and routing still look like the bottleneck—not fund yields.
•Stablecoins — Payments and reserve infrastructure was little changed (-0.7% market cap, 30D); a major reserve-fund launch led the week (a major institution launched a dedicated stablecoin reserve money market fund—reserve productization is moving beyond pilot banking relationships), suggesting reserve infrastructure is running ahead of the flat market-cap read.
•RWA tokenization — TradFi-to-on-chain bridge was little changed (-1.1% tokenized Treasuries, 30D); private-credit and distribution headlines drove the narrative (not a move in on-chain Treasury supply), even as crypto spot sold off.
•U.S. crypto ETFs — Spot ETF AUM softened (-24.2% aggregate AUM, 30D); crypto spot weakness hit AUM marks while 30-day net flows turned positive (+$442M)—watch whether allocators keep adding on the dip.
•Crypto prices — Crypto markets pulled back (-19.0% total market cap, 30D); on-chain rails were largely unchanged—suggesting macro and ETF flows, not rail fundamentals, drove the selloff.
